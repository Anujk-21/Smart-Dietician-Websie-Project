Diabetes Indian Meal Plan:


Breakfast:

Whole grain roti or paratha with vegetables (such as onion, tomato, and lettuce) Boiled or baked beans Low-fat curd or buttermilk


Mid-morning Snack:

Fresh fruit (such as apple, pear, or berries) Roasted chana or makhana


Lunch:

Whole grain rice or quinoa Lentil or vegetable-based curry (such as dal or sabji) Salad made with fresh vegetables (such as cucumber, carrot, and tomato) A bowl of curd


Evening Snack:

Fresh fruit juice or smoothie A handful of nuts (such as almonds, walnuts, or cashews) Peanut butter sandwich made with whole grain bread


Dinner:

Baked or grilled fish or chicken Steamed or roasted vegetables (such as brussels sprouts, broccoli, or carrots) A bowl of soup (such as vegetable or lentil)



It's important to keep in mind that portion sizes are just as important as the type of food you eat. To keep your blood sugar levels stable, aim to eat small, frequent meals throughout the day instead of three large meals. Additionally, limiting your intake of added sugars, saturated and trans fats, and sodium can also help manage blood sugar levels. It's always a good idea to work with a registered dietitian or a healthcare provider to create a personalized plan that fits your specific needs and health conditions.
