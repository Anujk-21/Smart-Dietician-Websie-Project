Hypertension Indian Meal Plan:


Breakfast:

Whole grain oats or porridge with milk and fruits (such as berries, banana, or apple) An egg (boiled or scrambled) A cup of green tea


Mid-morning Snack:

Fresh fruit (such as watermelon, muskmelon, or papaya) Roasted or sprouted seeds (such as flaxseeds, sunflower seeds, or pumpkin seeds)


Lunch:

Brown rice or quinoa Vegetable or lentil-based curry (such as chana masala or dal) A serving of leafy greens (such as spinach or kale) A bowl of low-fat curd


Evening Snack:

Fresh vegetable juice or smoothie A handful of nuts (such as almonds, walnuts, or cashews) Whole grain crackers with hummus


Dinner:

Grilled or baked chicken or fish Steamed or roasted vegetables (such as carrots, sweet potatoes, or Brussels sprouts) A bowl of vegetable soup



It's important to limit your intake of salt, saturated and trans fats, and added sugars. Additionally, incorporating plenty of fruits and vegetables, whole grains, lean protein sources, and healthy fats can help lower blood pressure and improve heart health. Aim to drink plenty of water and limit your intake of alcohol.
