UnderWeight (<18.5)

Indian diet plan for those with a BMI less 18.5:

Breakfast:

1 cup of milk or soy milk 1 banana 2 boiled eggs 2 slices of whole grain bread

Mid-morning Snack:

1 cup of fresh fruit salad

Lunch:

1 cup of mixed vegetables (carrots, beans, broccoli, etc.) cooked in 1 tsp of oil 1 small bowl of dal (lentil soup) 2-3 chapatis made with whole wheat flour 1 small bowl of curd

Evening Snack:

1 cup of roasted chana (chickpeas) or 1 boiled sweet potato

Dinner:

1 cup of brown rice or quinoa 1 small bowl of vegetable curry 1 small bowl of curd or raita

Before Bed:

1 glass of warm milk or soy milk

Remember to include plenty of protein-rich foods such as eggs, dal, and chana in your diet, as well as healthy sources of fat such as nuts and seeds. Also, aim to consume at least 5 servings of fruits and vegetables daily. You can adjust the portion sizes according to your individual needs and preferences. It is recommended that you consult a registered dietitian or doctor to determine the best diet plan for your specific needs.

Healthy (18.5 - 25)

Indian diet plan for those with a BMI between 18.5 and 25:

Breakfast:

1 cup of tea or coffee 2-3 idlis or 2-3 dosas made with whole wheat flour 1 small bowl of sambar or chutney

Mid-morning Snack:

1 medium-sized fruit such as an apple, pear or orange

Lunch:

1 small bowl of rice or quinoa 1 small bowl of dal 1 small bowl of vegetable curry 1 small bowl of curd or raita

Evening Snack:

1 cup of tea or coffee 2-3 biscuits or 1 small bowl of roasted chana

Dinner:

2-3 chapatis made with whole wheat flour 1 small bowl of vegetable curry 1 small bowl of dal 1 small bowl of curd or raita

Before Bed:

1 glass of warm milk or soy milk

Make sure to include plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins and healthy fats in your diet. Avoid processed and high-fat foods, and limit your intake of sugary drinks and snacks. Also, try to stay hydrated by drinking at least 8-10 glasses of water per day. Remember to adjust the portion sizes according to your individual needs and preferences. It is recommended that you consult a registered dietitian or doctor to determine the best diet plan for your specific needs.

OverWeight (25-30)

Indian diet plan for those with a BMI between 25 and 30:

Breakfast:

1 small bowl of oatmeal with low-fat milk or soy milk 1 small bowl of fruit salad

Mid-morning Snack:

1 cup of green tea 1 small bowl of sprouts or 1 small fruit

Lunch:

2-3 chapatis made with whole wheat flour 1 small bowl of brown rice or quinoa 1 small bowl of dal or lentil soup 1 small bowl of vegetable curry or stir-fry 1 small bowl of curd or raita

Evening Snack:

1 cup of tea or coffee 2-3 whole grain crackers or 1 small bowl of roasted chana

Dinner:

1 small bowl of vegetable soup or clear soup 1 small bowl of salad with a variety of colorful vegetables 1 small bowl of grilled or baked fish or chicken 1 small bowl of curd or raita

Before Bed:

1 glass of warm milk or soy milk

It is important to limit your calorie intake and increase physical activity to promote weight loss. Try to consume more protein-rich foods such as lentils, legumes, nuts, and seeds, while limiting your intake of processed foods, sugary drinks, and snacks. Incorporate more vegetables and fruits into your meals to increase fiber intake and promote satiety. Aim to engage in regular physical activity, such as brisk walking, jogging, cycling or swimming, for at least 30 minutes a day, most days of the week. Remember to adjust the portion sizes according to your individual needs and preferences. It is recommended that you consult a registered dietitian or doctor to determine the best diet plan for your specific needs.
